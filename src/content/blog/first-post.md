Sooo this feels surreal.

I'm actually in Ontario right now on a software engineering internship at IBM wooooo *clap clap* thank you, thank you, thank you very much.

Okay but seriously I'm still processing that I'm here. Not just at IBM, but in Ontario.

This has been my dream ever since I started university (in Alberta) about a year and a half ago. I was I guess enamoured by the vibrant tech scene of Toronto haha (*i still am*)

Just to recap some recent events:

**0x0** - I went to my first MLH (Major League Hacking) sponsored hackathon and won a track in a team of two just last weekend

**0x1** - I'm en route to deploy a cybersecurity-focused project for IBM this week

**0x2** - And if I have time after work, I'll be going to several of the events during Toronto Tech Week this May


But while this has been my dream for over a year now, I'm realizing now that no matter where I am in the pursuit for the next big thing, it doesn't fully define my happiness.

Over the past year, it's as if the span <sub>[0]</sub> of the vector that is me is always trying to realign itself with the next level of what I view as success. So much so I become a different person every iteration.


**0x0 -** Initially, getting into the CS
program at my university was a big deal.
But I didn't celebrate. It was more stress than celebration at the time.

**0x1 -** Landing an internship at a
small startup somehow skipped straight to
IBM, it was a similar story but mostly relief after a lot of effort.

Now? I could go all in on my next big
thing which I will always work towards despite whatever mental disposition I'm in, and that would certainly feed my sense of achievement. But I don't know that I could derive my happiness from that. I don't think I can.

Don't get me wrong, the opportunities I've
had and the gratitude I feel for them
undoubtedly contribute to my happiness. But as one of my close CS friends who grinds the hell out of leetcode says, "sometimes people forget to grow outside of it". And that's coming from a dude who grinds leetcode *for* **fun**. So I think it'd be wise for me to take his advice haha.

I don't know what's going to happen in the next 3–5 years, but what I know now is that I don't want to lose my span.

The part of me that enjoys things before they become goals.
The part that isn’t constantly optimizing for what's next.

I think I owe that version of myself a little more attention.
